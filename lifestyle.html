Lifestyle for healthy skin

How does your lifestyle affect your skin’s health?

There’s no doubt that the secret to clear, healthy skin is through your diet and lifestyle. You could have the world’s best skin care regime, but it would be all for nothing if you don’t take proper care of your skin. In actual fact, the perfect skincare routine is a balanced, healthy lifestyle and quality skin-nourishing products.

Nutrition and skin health

Vitamins and nutrients affect skin health directly. In particular, Vitamin A, C ,D, E and Zinc can be hugely beneficial to your complexion. It’s essential to be conscious about what you are eating and drinking to get the most out of your skin treatments. It's important to have a list of skin-safe foods in your mind in order to know what to eat when your skin needs some nourishment, so we have prepared the ultimate list for you!

Best superfoods for promoting skin health




sweet potatoes

Red and orange vegetables contain lycopene - a nutrient that helps prevent skin discoloration, texture changes, fine lines and wrinkles. Sweet potatoes are a delicious food that can boost your skin's overall health while keeping you full.




Oranges

Oranges are a well-known food source of vitamin C, they have been proven to stimulate the formation of collagen , improve skin hydration, and improve wound healing. Also, the smell of citrusy fruits like oranges has been proven to be relaxing.




Blueberries

Blueberries are rich in antioxidants and vitamins that can help boost the strength of collagen fibres in our skin and give it a healthy glow. Blueberries can be eaten alone or with a mix of other delicious berries to give your skin the elasticity it needs!




Avocados

Avocados are rich in monounsaturated fatty acids, which hydrate and shield the skin by lowering the risk of premature ageing caused by ultraviolet radiation. Avocados are really effective at increasing hydration levels in the skin and reducing inflammation. Ripe avocadoes can be mashed with other vegetables like tomatoes and herbs like basil to give you a boost of healthy energy.




Carrots

Carrots have beta carotene, a natural sun protectant, and are rich in vitamin A, which helps restore damaged collagen and is the main ingredient in skin products that contain retinol. Not only are carrots good for your eyesight, but they can help protect your skin against the sun's UV rays and keep your skin looking youthful.




Kale

Leafy greens are one of the most potent sources of vitamin K, a vitamin that helps with blood clotting and faster healing when eaten. Kale is also an excellent source of vitamin C which is great for brightening your skin’s complexion. Kale is a great vegetable for avoiding skin health problems that come with aging.




Almonds

Almonds are renowned for their skin healing properties. Almonds contain high amounts of Vitamin E which helps repair damaged cells and defends against the sun’s rays. Vitamin E also acts as an antioxidant, working to limit free radicals and also support the skin’s ageing process. It can also help promote a healthy, even skin tone.




Green tea

Green tea is packed with antioxidants that can rid your skin of any congestion and protect against damaging environmental stressors, leaving you with a glowing, healthy complexion. When you drink green tea, make sure to inhale the warm vapor from your cup to get all the stress relieving properties!

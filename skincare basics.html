Why it’s important to know your skin type

Your skin falls into one of four main categories: oily, combination, dry, or sensitive. Each type has unique characteristics and responds differently to environmental factors. Because skincare ingredients can benefit some skin types while potentially harming others, it’s essential to understand what works best for your skin and what doesn’t.

How to care for oily Skin

Oily skin overproduces an oily, waxy substance called sebum. Oily skin can be acne-prone, as pollutants and dead skin cells
could mix with the sebum and clog the skin’s pores which is the most common cause for pimples to appear. Here is a
skincare routine which focuses on reducing oily skin’s sebum production while keeping it healthy and moisturized.

  1. Cleanse Your face up to twice a day with an exfoliating foaming cleanser

    Look for a cleanser containing salicylic acid. It works by exfoliating the skin and unclogging pores. Salicylic acid also has anti-inflammatory properties that can help to reduce redness and swelling associated with acne. If you’re prone to acne, look for a facial cleanser that contains salicylic acid.

  2. Use an alcohol-free exfoliating toner to get rid of dead skin cells

    Lactic acid is an exfoliating alpha-hydroxy acid that helps promote the shedding of dead skin cells. By gently eliminating the build-up of dead cells, this ingredient helps soften and smooth your skin and improve its overall texture.

  3. Apply a lightweight, water-based moisturizer

    Make sure the moisturizer is oil-free and non-comedogenic in order to avoid getting clogged pores. Glycerin is a great ingredient to look for, as it’s a humectant, which means it helps to draw moisture into the skin.

  4. Make sure to apply sunscreen every 3 hours when outdoors

    Applying sunscreen frequently prevents sun damage that could lead to wrinkles, age spots, and even skin cancer. Use a lightweight, non-greasy, mineral sunscreen containing zinc oxide, an ingredient that reflects and scatters the sun’s UV rays while absorbing the skin’s oil. Make sure the sunscreen contains SPF 50+.

How to care for combination Skin

Having a combination skin type means that you have oily areas on your face such as your forehead, nose, and chin, but
also some dry areas such as your cheeks. Dealing with this skin type requires a balance between using products for oily
skin and dry skin. Here is a skincare routine which focuses on minimizing oiliness and dry patches to get a strong, well
nourished skin barrier!

  1. Cleanse your face up to twice a day with an exfoliating foaming cleanser

    Cleansers containing mandelic acid are great for combination skin. Mandelic acid is a gentler exfoliant compared to salicylic acid. It helps promote hydration and firmness of the skin while gently getting rid of dead skin cells.

  2. Use a niacinamide serum on oily areas of the face

    Niacinamide helps build keratin, a protein that maintains skin health. It’s also been shown to make your skin stronger, smoother, and brighter. Applying a niacinamide serum to oily areas on the face helps control the oiliness and texture of the skin.

  3. Use a retinol serum

    Retinol increases skin cell production (proliferation). It helps unclog pores, exfoliates your skin, and increases collagen production. This can reduce the appearance of fine lines and wrinkles, giving your skin a fresher, plump appearance.

  4. Use a moisturizer containing ceramides

    Ceramides are naturally occurring lipids in the skin that help to keep it hydrated and protected. Ceramides prevent skin dryness and irritation.

  5. Use a non-greasy SPF50+ sunscreen every 3 hours when outdoors

    Combination skin can be prone to hyperpigmentation. To prevent dark sun spots from appearing, make sure to apply enough sunscreen throughout the day.

How to care for dry skin

Dry skin makes the skin look and feel rough, itchy, flaky or scaly. The location where these dry patches form vary from person
to person. Dry skin can get irritated easily which makes your skin feel lots of discomfort when it doesn’t have enough moisture,
which is why focusing on moisturizing ingredients is key when dealing with dry skin. Here is a skincare routine which focuses
on minimizing oiliness and dry patches to get a strong, well nourished skin barrier!

  1. Cleanse twice a day with an oil cleanser

    Oil cleansers are formulated with lightweight plant oils, and they lather when combined with water. This allows oil cleansers to effectively cleanse away dirt and impurities without stripping or weighing down the skin, making them a great option for those with dry skin types.

  2. Use a green tea toner

    Green tea is a great antioxidant for your skin. It helps to calm down any dryness or irritation while keeping your skin looking youthful with its anti-ageing properties.

  3. Use a hyaluronic acid serum

    Hyaluronic acid helps skin stretch and flex and reduces skin wrinkles and lines. Hyaluronic acid is best used before a moisturizer as it works as a humectant, which traps moisture in the skin and keeps it from evaporating, especially in dry environments.

  4. Use a thick moisturizer rich in ceramides and hyaluronic acid

    The hydrating and repairing properties of ceramides, combined with the moisture-trapping hyaluronic acid in a thick moisturizer, is the key to keeping dry skin types looking supple and healthy.

  5. Make sure to apply a thick SPF 50+ sunscreen every 3 hours

    Dry skin is more prone to getting sunburnt, which could lead to even flakier skin and more irritation. Sunscreen is essential to maintaining the skin’s health.

How to care for sensitive Skin

Sensitive skin is a condition that makes your skin more prone to reactions such as redness and itching. Most people who
have sensitive skin notice occasional or frequent itching, burning, and stinging on patches of skin. It’s important to focus
on strengthening the skin barrier for sensitive skin to avoid getting more issues. Here is a skincare routine which focuses
on minimizing oiliness and dry patches to get a strong, well nourished skin barrier!

  1. Cleanse your face twice a day with a face wash containing squalane

    Squalane mimics the skin’s natural ability to repair and moisturize itself through sebum. It is an organic emollient that helps sensitive skin maintain hydration.

  2. Use rosehip oil

    Rosehips are rich in vitamin C, making rosehip oil effective for protecting and repairing the skin. It acts as an antioxidant, shielding cells from damage, reversing sun damage, and addressing signs of aging caused by excessive sun exposure.

  3. Use a hyaluronic acid serum

    Hyaluronic acid enhances skin elasticity and reduces wrinkles. Best used before moisturizer, it traps moisture in the skin, particularly useful in dry climates.

  4. Use a thick moisturizer rich in ceramides and hyaluronic acid

    Combining ceramides and hyaluronic acid in a moisturizer ensures hydration and repairs dry skin, leaving it supple and healthy.

  5. Apply a thick SPF 50+ sunscreen every 3 hours

    Dry skin is more prone to sunburn, causing flakiness and irritation. Regular sunscreen application is essential for maintaining skin health.

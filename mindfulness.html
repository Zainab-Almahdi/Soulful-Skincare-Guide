Mindfulness for healthy skin

How does stress affect your skin?

When you feel stressed, your body releases a hormone called cortisol, this breaks down the collagen and elastin in skin, which can speed up the ageing process causing more fine lines and wrinkles. Cortisol also triggers increased oil production which can clog pores and lead to acne breakouts. Fortunately, there are lots of meditation techniques we can use to prevent or manage stress during difficult times.

Meditation for skin health

Meditation reduces stress, lowering cortisol levels that can lead to acne, inflammation, and aging.
It boosts circulation, balances oil production, and promotes a natural glow— resulting in healthier, clearer skin. When you meditate, you may clear away the information overload that builds up every day and contributes to your stress. Here are the best meditation techniques that effectively reduce stress!

Focused breathing

Sometimes, life can get overwhelming to the point where we forget to take deep breathes.
Not taking inhales and exhales deep enough increases feelings of anxiety, often making your chest feel heavy.
Luckily, focused breathing is the solution to get this weight off your chest.

    Focused breathing involves sitting quietly and focusing on your breath, here is how to do it:
  1. Take a comfortable seat, keeping your spine straight.
  2. Observe the natural inhale and exhale of your breath.
  3. Start to pay extra attention to the gaps — the spaces between your inhales and exhales. Notice them start to expand.
  4. Try to keep your body very still and breathe softly.
  5. If your mind wanders, come back to simply observing the breath.

Mindfulness meditation

Sitting in silence and being minful of your surrounding is the best medicine for when your thoughts get too loud,
making you feel like you are not in control of a situation or outcome. Mindful meditation brings you back into the moment
and lets you take back control!

    This type of meditation involves being present with your thoughts and emotions, helping you to become an observer of your thinking patterns.
  1. Either sit in stillness or practice during a repetitive activity such as knitting
  2. Focus on your breath, or on each movement of the activity. Focus on the breath, or focus on the activity with intent.
  3. Repeat several times, or set an alarm for a specific amount of time.

Box breathing exercise

If you feel anxious to the point where focused breathing is not quite helping in clearing your
mind, box breathing is a great technique which can effectively get you out of your "fight or flight"
nervous system response and get you to relax.

    Box breathing can help in those moments when stress takes over and you need an immediate way to relax your mind and body.
  1. Inhale for 4 seconds.
  2. Hold the breath for 4 seconds.
  3. Exhale for 4 seconds.
  4. Hold the breath for 4 seconds.
  5. Repeat 12 to 15 times or more as needed, and don’t skimp on the “holds.” Those gentle pause points between inhalations and exhalations help cue the nervous system into relaxing.

Walking meditation

Getting out of the place you have been spending your time in while being stressed can be beneficial for stress management.
Taking a walk out in nature and taking deep breaths of fresh air will help get you back into the moment, leaving
any looming negative bad thoughts behind you.

    As the name suggests, you practice this meditation while walking.
  1. Find a path that you want to use for your meditation. Consider starting with a short route.
  2. Begin walking on this path. Slow and steady is key.
  3. Focus on the physical feeling of each step, and perhaps let your steps fall into a rhythm with your breath.
  4. Consider starting small, such as 5 minutes, then increasing this time as you get used to the practice.

4-7-8 breathing technique

A common side effect of overthing is having a lack of sleep, which consequently increases stress. A great way to
prevent racing negative thoughts before bed is to clear your mind with a breathing exercise while counting in a
numerical sentence, so the 4-7-8 breathing technique is a perfect fix!

    The 4-7-8 breathing technique involves breathing in for 4 seconds, holding the breath for 7 seconds, and exhaling for 8 seconds.
    This breathing pattern aims to reduce anxiety or help people get to sleep.
  1. empty your lungs of air.
  2. Breathe in quietly through your nose for 4 seconds.
  3. Hold your breathe for a count of 7 seconds.
  4. exhaling forcefully through the mouth, pursing the lips, and making a “whoosh” sound for 8 seconds
  5. repeat the cycle up to 4 times
